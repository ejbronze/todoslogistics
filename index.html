Why the Caribbean

A Region With Structural Trade Needs

The Caribbean is heavily import-dependent, with limited domestic manufacturing capacity and strong demand for coordinated logistics infrastructure. Most Caribbean nations rely on external sourcing for a significant portion of their consumable and industrial goods.

At the same time, cross-border trade ‚Äî particularly between the Dominican Republic and Haiti ‚Äî remains one of the region's most commercially active but logistically underserved corridors. This is where TODOS Logistics is positioned to have the greatest impact.

The Dominican Republic's strategic position ‚Äî at the center of Caribbean shipping lanes, adjacent to Haiti, and well-connected to U.S. and European trade routes ‚Äî makes it an ideal base for a coordination-focused logistics operator.

We understand the regulatory environment, the operational challenges, and the institutional requirements of this market in a way that general logistics providers typically do not.
